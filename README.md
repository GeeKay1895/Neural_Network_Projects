# 📌 Project 1: Gas Turbine Energy Yield Prediction

---

## 🎯 Objective

To predict Turbine Energy Yield (TEY) using:
- ambient variables
- turbine sensor measurements
- temperature and pressure features

through Artificial Neural Networks.

---

## 📂 Dataset Description

The dataset contains gas turbine sensor and ambient variables including:

- Ambient Temperature (AT)
- Ambient Pressure (AP)
- Ambient Humidity (AH)
- Turbine Inlet Temperature (TIT)
- Compressor Discharge Pressure (CDP)
- Gas Turbine Exhaust Pressure (GTEP)
- Turbine After Temperature (TAT)
- Carbon Monoxide (CO)
- Nitrogen Oxides (NOx)
- Turbine Energy Yield (TEY)

---

## 🔍 Business Problem

Power plants and industrial systems require accurate prediction of energy output for:
- performance optimization
- predictive monitoring
- operational efficiency
- energy management

---

## 🧠 Deep Learning Approach

An Artificial Neural Network (ANN) regression model was developed using:
- TensorFlow
- Keras Sequential API
- Dense hidden layers
- ReLU activation functions

---

## 📊 Model Evaluation

The ANN model successfully learned relationships between:
- sensor variables
- ambient conditions
- turbine performance

Evaluation Metrics:
- R² Score
- RMSE

---

## 💡 Business Insights

- Sensor variables strongly influence turbine performance.
- Deep learning captures complex industrial patterns effectively.
- Predictive systems improve energy optimization strategies.

---

## 🚀 Business Impact

The project supports:
- Industrial analytics
- Smart energy systems
- Predictive maintenance
- Power plant optimization

# 📌 Project 2: Forest Fire Burned Area Prediction

---

## 🎯 Objective

To predict the burned area of forest fires using:
- environmental variables
- weather conditions
- seasonal factors

through Artificial Neural Networks.

---

## 📂 Dataset Description

The dataset contains:
- Temperature
- Humidity
- Wind
- Rain
- Month
- Day
- Burned Area

---

## 🔍 Environmental Problem

Wildfire prediction is important for:
- disaster management
- environmental monitoring
- fire prevention planning
- resource allocation

---

## 🧠 Deep Learning Approach

An Artificial Neural Network regression model was developed using:
- TensorFlow
- Keras
- Dense neural layers
- Feature scaling
- Target variable transformation

---

## 📈 Model Improvement

The target variable (burned area) showed heavy skewness.

A logarithmic transformation was applied to:
- stabilize variance
- improve learning performance
- reduce prediction instability

This significantly improved model performance.

---

## 📊 Final Model Performance

- R² Score: 0.51
- RMSE: 1.03

---

## 💡 Environmental Insights

- Temperature and wind strongly influence wildfire behavior.
- Forest fire prediction contains highly non-linear relationships.
- Neural Networks help model complex environmental systems.

---

## 🚀 Environmental Impact

The project supports:
- Wildfire risk analysis
- Disaster preparedness
- Environmental analytics
- Fire management systems
